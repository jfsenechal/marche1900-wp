# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The public website for **Marche 1900**, the traditional heritage market held each August in Marche-en-Famenne, Belgium (marché aux oiseaux, artisanat & jeux folkloriques, associations, fireworks). The site is fully bilingual: French is the primary language, Dutch (NL) is maintained at parity. Three audiences:

- **General public / visitors** — local and regional families and tourists deciding whether and how to attend, then looking up practical details (dates, program, access).
- **Participating associations** — the local clubs and asbl who run stands and activities, who need their presence represented.
- **Press & media** — journalists and outlets covering the event (dedicated `pers` / press area and `medias` / media pages).

## Product Purpose

Two jobs, weighted equally:

1. **Help people plan a visit** — surface the dates, program, access/parking, and practical info clearly enough that a visitor can decide to come and know how to get there.
2. **Convey the event's character** — communicate the heritage and atmosphere of Marche 1900 so the site builds excitement and emotional connection, not just logistics.

Success looks like a visitor arriving on any page (in FR or NL), immediately understanding what the event is and when it happens, and being able to act on it (plan the visit, find their association, or reach the press contact).

## Brand Personality

**Heritage & authentic.** Rooted in tradition and 1900s craft; warm, timeless, with Belle Époque character that feels genuine rather than staged. Voice is welcoming and local, never corporate or salesy. Bilingual copy (FR/NL) must read naturally in both languages, not as machine translation.

Three words: **authentic, warm, timeless.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / template look** — no cream-background, gradient-and-identical-card-grid AI-startup aesthetic; nothing that reads as a website-builder template.
- **Dated / cluttered** — avoid the busy early-2010s feel of the current Twenty Thirteen base: cramped layouts, small text, heavy decorative textures.
- **Corporate / cold** — no sterile corporate blue or generic business stock photography with no local character.
- **Theme-park kitsch** — no costume-drama clichés: faux parchment, ornate gold scrollwork, or fake-vintage gimmicks. Heritage is carried by real photography, typography, and restraint, not props.

## Design Principles

1. **Authenticity over costume.** Evoke 1900s heritage through genuine craft (real event photography, considered typography, materials with character), never through literal vintage gimmicks.
2. **Practical clarity first.** A visitor's core questions (what, when, where, how to get there) are always one glance away; atmosphere never buries logistics.
3. **Bilingual parity.** FR and NL are equal citizens. Any change to a page must hold up in both languages, including layout that flexes for different text lengths.
4. **Local warmth, not corporate polish.** The tone is a welcoming community celebration, friendly and human, not a brand campaign.
5. **Quiet confidence.** Let strong photography and generous space do the work; resist clutter and decoration for their own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any motion, and comfortably readable type sizes for a broad, all-ages family audience.
